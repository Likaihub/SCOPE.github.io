SCOPE: Skeleton Graph-Based Computation-Efficient Framework for Autonomous UAV Exploration

Video

Abstract

Autonomous exploration in unknown environments is key for mobile robots, helping them perceive, map, and make decisions in complex, large areas. However, current methods still struggle with problems of high computational costs and inefficient global guidance, especially in real-world settings. To address these limitations, we propose a novel autonomous exploration framework named as SCOPE. It incrementally constructs a real-time skeletal graph to represent the spatial environment for guiding subsequent planning. The planning layer of the framework comprises two main components: the Proximal Planner, which efficiently generates exploration routes in the vicinity of the UAV, and the Region-Sequence Planner, which optimizes the visitation order across the entire environment. Comparative evaluations in simulation environments demonstrate that our method maintains exploration efficiency while reducing computational cost by an average of 86.9\% compared to baseline approaches. Real-world experiments further validate the effectiveness of our method in practical scenarios. We will release the source code to the community.

System Framework

SCOPE is an autonomous exploration framework that builds a skeletal graph of free space to understand 3D structure and obstacle distribution. It analyzes unknown regions to identify key exploration targets and plan efficiently. The proximal planner focuses on local exploration and isolated areas, while the region-sequence planner provides global guidance based on the skeletal graph, achieving efficient large-scale exploration.

Simulation Experiment

SCOPE demonstrates superior exploration efficiency in complex environments, achieving faster coverage with significantly lower computational cost. In the Complex Office scenario, it reduces exploration time by 10.9% and computation by 78.8% compared to state-of-the-art methods, sustaining smooth and stable flight without redundant maneuvers.

Real-World Experiments

We evaluated SCOPE on a real UAV platform to verify its feasibility for autonomous exploration. The UAV was equipped with an onboard Jetson Orin NX for computation, an Intel T265 for real-time pose estimation, an Intel D435i for depth sensing, and a PX4 flight controller for motion control.

Experiment Results

SCOPE was tested in multiple real-world environments, ranging from narrow mock-up passages to cluttered offices with dense furniture and complex occlusions. The framework effectively guided the UAV through tight spaces, prioritized hard-to-reach regions, and adapted to unstructured layouts. Across all scenarios, it maintained smooth navigation, comprehensive coverage, and low computational overhead, demonstrating robust performance in challenging real-world conditions.
